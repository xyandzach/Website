Console Timeline

PS One

The PS One was the first console I ever played on. Given to me by my Dad, I remember staying up on countless school nights playing Crash Team Racing. I am happy the console that started the iconic PlayStation console series was the console that started my gaming career as well.

2000

Xbox

The first console that I spent a considerable amount. Mostly playing single player games like Halo 1 and 2. Nothing would beat having friends over on a weekend night to play Burnout, Star Wars Battlefront, and GTA. Memory cards would be a thing of the past for me from this point on in gaming.

2001

Game Boy Advance SP

My first handheld console! The Game Boy SP showed me a whole new side of gaming. I was no longer locked to the couch and had the ability to game anywhere I went.

2003

Nintendo DS

After the amazing experience I had with the Game Boy SP, I decide that my next console would be another handheld. Although the Nintendo DS would be as great of a console for me as the GB SP, it was never the less a vital part of my gaming upbringing. Having two screens and a stylus, the DS felt alien to me at the time and was the first piece of technology that peaked my interest as to how it was put together and operated. Definitely something that helped kick start my general interest in tech.

2004

Xbox 360

Another revolutionary console for me, the Xbox 360 was the first console to expose me to online multiplayer. Although I had a few online experiences with my Xbox and DS, Xbox 360 games like Halo 3, Call of Duty: Modern Warfare 2, and Red Dead Redemption made it so I was spending more time playing online then offline.

2005

Alienwate M11x

My first PC! After nearly melting my Moms laptop with browser games and begging my parents for months for a computer of my own, I was given an Alienware M11x for my birthday. Having a PC of my own opened me up to brand new experiences in gaming. PC only games like World of Warcraft and Half Life gave me a whole new take on how games could be played.

2011

Custom PC

A proud accomplishment of mine, my custom built PC. I built my PC in 2012 when I was age 15. Back then there were far fewer resources on PC parts and building online, and no one I knew besides a few friends had any knowledge PC building. After years of tinkering and hot swapping parts, as well as a recent GPU upgrade on loan, my PC is still able to keep up with the latest releases and continues to give me great experiences in gaming.

2012
